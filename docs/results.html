1 Summary

This page presents the results of analysis that have been done. Every section is one disease (phenotype of interest) and each subsection is the analysis for a particular intermediate trait.

Every subsection presents the following information.

  • Comparison between raw \(\beta\) and posterior mean of \(\beta\) for computing PRS (This is not available now)
  • Marginal association:
    • \(Y \sim I\)
    • \(Y \sim G_i\)
    • \(Y \sim I + G_i\)
  • Interaction test results based on functional subsetting
  • Follow up comment on the results (functional subsetting) if any
  • Interaction test results based on significance subsetting (if run)
  • Follow up comment on the results (significance subsetting) if any
  • Gene-based analysis
    • Marginal effect
    • Interaction test

This paper shows the strong genetic correlation between coronary heart disease and LDL and conditional FDR (on LDL; namely incorporating LDL p-value into FDR calculation) of CHD help to identify more signal out of GWAS follow-up study. Also, LDL has been shown to be causal factor of CHD, paper.

rs13465 locates in the intronic region of ILF3. Its protective effect is masked by high LDL level.

Interestingly, this paper reported that ILF3 (rs2569512 was used to tag the gene) affects myocardial infarction risk only when LDL level is low (when LDL level is high, there is no association signal).

Here, I list SNPs that have genome-wide significant p-value in GWAS and interaction p-value < 0.1

rs564398 locates in the intronic region of CDKN2B-AS1 (CDKN2B antisense RNA 1). It is a known CHD locus link. It protective effect goes away when LDL is high but there is no strong correlation between the locus and LDL level.

rs7049105 also locates in CDKN2B-AS1 (intronic region). Its risk goes away when LDL level is high. This direction is also consistent with the previous two loci.

listGeneBasedTest(config.ldl)

Gene wildcards used for search are ILF3,TMEM132D,CDKN2B

  1. The marginal effect of \(E\) on \(Y\) and \(I\) is here
  2. The interaction test: \(Y \sim I + E + I \times E\) is here

Only ILF3-AS1 and TMEM132D has predicted expression in the selected tissue types. Interestingly, The expression level of ILF3-AS1 does associated with phenotype in some tissue types (Esophagus, Thyroid, etc) and its effects on LDL are consistent with the effect on CHD (this is also as expected since LDL is correlated with CHD). The interaction term is not significant, but the direction are the same across all tissues. That is the interaction tends to mask the effect of ILF3-AS1 expression when LDL is high. This result is also consistent with the previous finding in functionally consistent SNPs.

For TMEM132D, neither marginal nor interaction term is significant, which indicates that TMEM132D may not be the target gene of the associated SNPs.

4.2.3 Gene-based analysis

listGeneBasedTest(config.fastinsulin)

Gene wildcards used for search are RAB38,UHRF1,ANKS1A,HRH1,LGR5,TSPAN8

  1. The marginal effect of \(E\) on \(Y\) and \(I\) is here
  2. The interaction test: \(Y \sim I + E + I \times E\) is here

All of them have some predicted expression but only ANKS1A has significant interaction (p < 0.05, in whole blood). When fast insulin is high, the expression of ANKS1A enhances the risk though it has marginal effect to decrease the level of risk factor, fast insulin. An explanation is that ANKS1A should be maintained at certain level to keep fast insulin from being too high (this explains why ANKS1A expression decreases fast insulin level) and if baseline fast insulin is high, such protective effect is masked. ANKS1A has been associated with coronary heart disease and cancer. Note that ANKS1A is close to UHRF1BP1 and this locus has been associated with fast insulin paper. However, ANKS1A has not been identified as a T2D locus.
