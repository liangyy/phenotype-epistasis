Results

28 February, 2018

1 Summary

This page presents the results of analysis that have been done. Every section is one disease (phenotype of interest) and each subsection is the analysis for a particular intermediate trait.

Every subsection presents the following information.

  • Comparison between raw \(\beta\) and posterior mean of \(\beta\) for computing PRS
  • Marginal association:
    • \(Y \sim I\)
    • \(Y \sim G_i\)
    • \(Y \sim I + G_i\)
  • Interaction test results based on functional subsetting
  • Follow up comment on the results if any

This paper shows the strong genetic correlation between coronary heart disease and LDL and conditional FDR (on LDL; namely incorporating LDL p-value into FDR calculation) of CHD help to identify more signal out of GWAS follow-up study. Also, LDL has been shown to be causal factor of CHD, paper.

rs13465 locates in the intronic region of ILF3. Its protective effect is masked by high LDL level.

drawGraph('-', '-', '+', '+', 'cad-ldl') #

Interestingly, this paper reported that ILF3 (rs2569512 was used to tag the gene) affects myocardial infarction risk only when LDL level is low (when LDL level is high, there is no association signal).

This paper proposed that triglycerides level is also a causal factor of CHD, paper.

rs4541125 shows most significant interaction (positive: i.e. as \(I\) increases, the effect size of \(G\) increases). In other word, the protective effect of \(G\) goes away when \(I\) is high. It locates in the intronic region of PITPNM3, which is reported to be under-presented in the proteomics of Acute Ischemic Strokes patients (table 3 of the paper). Note that the triglycerides level is high in cases versus controls.

rs2139980. It direction is not the same as others.
